Scientific Validation & Research

Evidence-based personalized nutrition backed by clinical research and validated methodologies

Our Research Methodology

Lab1's approach combines genomic analysis, biomarker testing, and evidence-based supplementation strategies validated through peer-reviewed research.

Genomic Analysis: SNP-Based Nutrient Requirements

We analyze 40+ single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) associated with nutrient metabolism, including:

  • MTHFR (C677T, A1298C): Folate metabolism and methylation capacity
  • VDR (FokI, BsmI): Vitamin D receptor function and calcium absorption
  • FUT2: B12 absorption and gut microbiome composition
  • BCMO1: Beta-carotene to vitamin A conversion efficiency
  • CYP1A2: Caffeine metabolism and antioxidant requirements

Reference: Fenech et al. (2011). "Nutrigenetics and nutrigenomics: viewpoints on the current status and applications in nutrition research and practice." Journal of Nutrigenetics and Nutrigenomics.

Blood Biomarker Assessment

Comprehensive analysis of 35+ biomarkers across multiple health domains:

  • Nutritional Status: Vitamins (D, B12, folate), minerals (iron, magnesium, zinc), omega-3 index
  • Metabolic Health: HbA1c, fasting glucose, lipid panel, homocysteine
  • Inflammation & Oxidative Stress: hs-CRP, oxidized LDL
  • Hormonal Balance: Thyroid panel (TSH, fT3, fT4), cortisol, testosterone

All testing performed in ISO 15189 accredited laboratories using validated assays.

Personalization Algorithm

Our proprietary algorithm integrates multiple data sources:

  1. Genetic predispositions from SNP analysis
  2. Current nutritional status from blood biomarkers
  3. Lifestyle factors (diet, exercise, stress, sleep)
  4. Health goals and existing conditions

The algorithm calculates optimal dosages based on documented nutrient interactions, absorption rates, and therapeutic ranges established in clinical literature.

Clinical Evidence for Personalized Nutrition

Key studies demonstrating the efficacy of genotype-tailored supplementation

MTHFR Variants & Folate Supplementation

Finding: Individuals with MTHFR C677T variants show 30-70% reduced folate enzyme activity, requiring methylfolate over folic acid for optimal homocysteine management.

Frosst et al. (1995). Nature Genetics; Gilbody et al. (2007). American Journal of Epidemiology.

Vitamin D Receptor Polymorphisms

Finding: VDR FokI variants influence calcium absorption efficiency and bone density response to vitamin D supplementation, with FF genotype requiring higher doses.

Uitterlinden et al. (2004). Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism; Gross et al. (1996). Journal of Bone and Mineral Research.

Omega-3 Response & APOE Genotype

Finding: APOE ε4 carriers show differential cardiovascular response to EPA/DHA supplementation compared to non-carriers, supporting genotype-based dosing strategies.

Carvalho-Wells et al. (2010). British Journal of Nutrition; Minihane et al. (2007). Proceedings of the Nutrition Society.

Iron Absorption & HFE Mutations

Finding: HFE C282Y and H63D variants affect iron absorption and storage, requiring modified supplementation protocols to prevent overload or deficiency.

Feder et al. (1996). Nature Genetics; Bacon et al. (2011). Hepatology.

Understanding Key Biomarkers

Why we test what we test: the clinical relevance of our biomarker panel

Vitamin D (25-OH-D)

Why it matters: Regulates calcium metabolism, immune function, and gene expression. Deficiency (<50 nmol/L) associated with increased risk of osteoporosis, autoimmune disease, and mortality.

Optimal range: 75-125 nmol/L (30-50 ng/mL)

Omega-3 Index

Why it matters: Percentage of EPA+DHA in red blood cell membranes. Levels >8% associated with 90% reduction in sudden cardiac death risk vs <4%.

Optimal range: >8% (cardioprotective zone)

Homocysteine

Why it matters: Marker of methylation capacity and B-vitamin status. Elevated levels (>10 μmol/L) linked to cardiovascular disease, cognitive decline, and neural tube defects.

Optimal range: <7 μmol/L

hs-CRP (High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein)

Why it matters: Sensitive marker of systemic inflammation. Levels >3 mg/L associated with 2x cardiovascular risk. Responds to omega-3, antioxidants, and anti-inflammatory nutrients.

Optimal range: <1 mg/L

HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)

Why it matters: 3-month average blood glucose. Levels >5.7% indicate prediabetes. Even within "normal" range, higher values predict diabetes risk. Modified by chromium, alpha-lipoic acid, berberine.

Optimal range: <5.4%

Ferritin (Iron Storage)

Why it matters: Both deficiency (<30 ng/mL) and excess (>300 ng/mL) harmful. Women often deficient; men and post-menopausal women risk overload. Requires genotype-informed supplementation (HFE status).

Optimal range: 30-100 ng/mL (women), 50-150 ng/mL (men)

Testing & Quality Standards

Laboratory Accreditation

All blood biomarker testing performed in ISO 15189 accredited medical laboratories meeting international standards for accuracy, precision, and quality control. DNA analysis conducted in CLIA-certified and CAP-accredited laboratories.

Ingredient Sourcing & Forms

We use exclusively bioavailable, clinically studied forms:

  • Methylfolate (L-5-MTHF) instead of folic acid (for MTHFR variants)
  • Methylcobalamin/Adenosylcobalamin instead of cyanocobalamin (B12)
  • Magnesium glycinate/threonate instead of oxide (superior absorption)
  • Vitamin K2 (MK-7) with documented half-life advantages
  • Algae-derived triglyceride EPA/DHA (superior to ethyl esters)

Third-Party Verification

  • Heavy metal testing (lead, mercury, cadmium, arsenic)
  • Microbiological contamination screening
  • Potency verification (active ingredient content)
  • Identity testing (confirming ingredient authenticity)
